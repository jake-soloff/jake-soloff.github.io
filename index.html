Jake A. Soloff

I am a postdoctoral researcher working with Rina Foygel Barber and Rebecca Willett at the University of Chicago. My research focuses on large-scale statistical inference: I get particularly excited about revisiting the conceptual foundations in order to develop new principles and methods. I have worked on high-dimensional statistics without assuming sparsity, incentive-compatible statistical inference, and provably controlling the local false discovery rate in multiple testing. My recent work focuses on mechanisms for guaranteeing stability of black-box machine learning models.

I obtained a Ph.D. in Statistics from UC Berkeley, co-advised by Aditya Guntuboyina and Michael I. Jordan. Previously, I received my Sc.B. in Mathematics from Brown University.
